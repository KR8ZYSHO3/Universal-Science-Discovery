**Title (for GitHub Discussion):**

USDR Early Stewards / Advisors — Call for Interest (June 2026 Launch Sprint)

**Body:**

We're preparing the public launch of the Universal Science Discovery Repository (USDR) — an open, git-native, version-controlled catalog of science's unknowns, cross-domain mathematical bridges, and falsifiable hypotheses.

**Current scale (verified at launch):**
- 1,124 cross-domain bridges across 55+ disciplines
- 1,409 open unknowns (0 orphans maintained)
- 1,275 testable hypotheses
- 3,861-node / 4,522-edge interactive knowledge graph
- Live OpenAlex + PubMed + Semantic Scholar automation feeding the system
- Preprint (v1.2 Launch Edition) ready for arXiv

To move from solo-seeded foundation to genuine community-owned infrastructure, we are calling for the **first cohort of Early Stewards / Launch Advisors** (target: 8–15 people in this initial wave).

### What the role actually is
- Light, high-signal advisory work in your domain(s)
- Review 1–2 PRs or issues per month (async)
- Occasionally propose high-leverage unknowns or bridge opportunities
- Give input on quality standards, roadmap priorities, and ethics
- (Optional but encouraged) Seed a small number of high-quality entries yourself

**Time commitment is deliberately capped**: 1–3 hours per month on average. This is not a heavy maintainer role. It's designed for working researchers who care about cross-domain discovery but cannot commit huge time.

### Who we're looking for
Researchers (any career stage) who:
- Have real depth in one or more scientific domains
- See the value in structured, version-controlled maps of what we *don't* know
- Want to help shape a long-term piece of scientific public infrastructure from the ground up

We especially want voices from physics, biology/medicine, complex systems, network science, computational fields, and open science practice.

### What you get
- Named credit in the arXiv preprint, CHANGELOG, and future "State of USDR" reports
- Entry in the project's pioneer lineage system
- Early, direct influence on governance and priorities (see GOVERNANCE.md)
- Private channel with the core team + priority invitation to the first virtual Unknowns Hackathon
- Public recognition as a founding external contributor
- The satisfaction of helping build something that could meaningfully accelerate discovery for decades

### How to express interest (low friction)
Reply to this post with:
- Your primary field(s)
- One or two sentences on why cross-domain / "unknowns" infrastructure matters to you
- Rough availability (e.g., "1–2 hours/month is realistic")

You can also email or DM if you prefer.

We will do rolling, lightweight selection. First cohort will be announced publicly with credit.

This is one of the highest-leverage moments to get involved in open scientific discovery infrastructure. If you've ever thought "someone should build a proper system for tracking what we don't know," this is your chance to help actually build it.
