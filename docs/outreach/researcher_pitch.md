# USDR Researcher Pitch

## The One-Sentence Pitch

The Universal Science Discovery Repository is a version-controlled, AI-augmented
knowledge engine that tracks what humanity *doesn't yet know* — and makes cross-domain
connections visible that no single researcher can see.

---

## Why This Matters to You

[Name], your work on [THEIR FIELD] touches on open problems that connect directly to
[BRIDGE DOMAIN]. The USDR currently has [N] open unknowns in your area, including:

- [SPECIFIC UNKNOWN 1]
- [SPECIFIC UNKNOWN 2]
- [SPECIFIC UNKNOWN 3]

These are linked to cross-domain bridges connecting your field to [OTHER FIELD], where
the same mathematics appears to govern both systems.

---

## What We're Building

- **603** structured catalog entries across **22** scientific domains
- **49** cross-domain bridges with formal mathematical translations
- A knowledge graph of **603 nodes** and **628 edges**
- An AI co-pilot that proposes novel bridge candidates by analysing domain connectivity gaps
- Open source, git-native, CC-BY licensed — peer-reviewable via pull requests

---

## What We Need from Domain Experts

1. **Validate our unknowns** — are these real open problems in your field?
2. **Propose new unknowns** — what questions keep you up at night?
3. **Review bridges** — does the cross-domain translation hold mathematically?
4. **Become a domain steward** — own the quality of one domain's content

Domain stewards are credited in the repository and on the dashboard. We follow a
lightweight governance model: all changes are versioned, peer-reviewed via GitHub pull
requests, and linked to primary literature.
