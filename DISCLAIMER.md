## Purpose of This Repository

The **Universal Science Discovery Repository (USDR)** is an open-source, community-driven project whose mission is to accelerate scientific discovery by:

- Creating a living, version-controlled catalog of scientific knowledge
- Explicitly surfacing and prioritizing the most important **unknowns** in science
- Providing structured, testable **hypotheses** that researchers can use as starting points
- Enabling cross-disciplinary connections and serendipitous insights
- Supporting reproducible experiments and collaborative research

This repository is intended as a **tool to accelerate discovery**, not as a final authority on scientific truth.

## Scientific Uncertainty & Risk

Science inherently involves uncertainty. Many entries in this repository represent:
- Active areas of research with competing theories
- Speculative hypotheses with varying levels of evidence
- Open questions where the answer is genuinely unknown

Users should approach all content with appropriate scientific skepticism and rigorous verification.

Some hypotheses may touch on **dual-use research areas** (technologies that could have both beneficial and harmful applications). Such entries are clearly labeled. Users are expected to follow all applicable ethical guidelines and legal regulations in their jurisdiction.
